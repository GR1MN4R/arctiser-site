Decision-making

Decisions follow the association’s statutes and documented routines to reduce ambiguity and protect independence.

  • Board meetings are documented (agenda + minutes).
  • Major changes (purpose, governance, finances) require formal approval.
  • Public-facing statements and publications follow review rules.

Integrity and independence

ARCTISER protects research integrity by separating funding from conclusions and documenting methodology.

  • No sponsor may influence research conclusions.
  • Methodology and limitations are disclosed in publications.
  • Conflicts of interest are declared and managed.

Conflicts of interest

Anyone involved in governance or research must disclose relevant interests that could bias decisions or results.

  • Financial interests (contracts, ownership, paid roles)
  • Close personal relationships relevant to a decision
  • Prior involvement that could compromise neutrality

Transparency

ARCTISER publishes key documents and updates as part of normal operations.

  • Statutes (vedtekter) and governance overview
  • High-level funding sources (when relevant)
  • Publications and archived research PDFs

Complaints and corrections

If a publication contains errors, ARCTISER documents and publishes corrections transparently.

  • Corrections: logged on the relevant research page / archive entry
  • Complaints: handled through board review when necessary

Data protection and privacy

ARCTISER minimizes personal data collection. If contact forms, newsletters, or analytics are used, a short privacy notice will describe what is collected and why.

  • Access limited to operational need
