Governance

This page describes how ARCTISER is organized, how decisions are made, and how integrity, transparency, and institutional independence are protected across research and operations.

Transparency Independence Methodological Integrity

Organizational structure

ARCTISER is organized as a Norway-registered, non-profit association with a defined purpose, statutes (vedtekter), and a board responsible for oversight and accountability.

Decision-making and documentation

Decisions follow the association’s statutes and documented routines to reduce ambiguity, ensure traceability, and protect institutional independence.

  • Board meetings are documented (agenda + minutes).
  • Major changes (purpose, governance, finances) require formal approval.
  • Public-facing statements and publications follow internal review rules.

Independence, conflicts of interest, and corrections

ARCTISER maintains separation between funding sources and analytical conclusions. Anyone involved in governance or research must disclose relevant interests that could bias decisions or results.

  • No sponsor may influence research conclusions.
  • Methodology and limitations are disclosed in publications.
  • Conflicts of interest are declared and managed.
  • Errors are corrected transparently through published corrections.
Contact: research@arctiser.no

Data protection and privacy

ARCTISER aims to minimize personal data collection. If contact forms, newsletters, or analytics are used, a short privacy notice will describe what is collected, why it is collected, and how long it is retained.

Principle: data minimization by default
Access: limited to operational need
Retention: only as long as necessary
