Statutes (Vedtekter)

This page provides the governing statutes of ARCTISER – Arctic Institute for Independent Social Research. These statutes define structure, authority, responsibilities, and operational principles.

§1 Name

The association’s name is ARCTISER – Arctic Institute for Independent Social Research. It is registered in Norway as a non-profit association.

§2 Purpose

ARCTISER exists to conduct, publish, and preserve independent research related to Arctic and broader societal structures, with emphasis on transparency, methodological rigor, and public relevance.

§3 Activities

  • Conduct research and analytical projects.
  • Publish reports, briefs, and archived materials.
  • Apply for grants that do not compromise independence.
  • Host events, workshops, or public knowledge initiatives.

§4 Governance

  • The association is governed by a board (Styret).
  • The board ensures compliance with statutes and ethical standards.
  • Major decisions require documented approval.

§5 Financial principles

  • ARCTISER operates on a non-profit basis.
  • Any surplus shall be used to further the association’s purpose.
  • Financial documentation shall follow good governance practice.

§6 Amendments

Amendments to these statutes require formal approval in accordance with internal governance rules and applicable Norwegian regulations.

§7 Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, remaining assets shall be transferred to a non-profit purpose aligned with ARCTISER’s mission.
