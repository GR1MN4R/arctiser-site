Statutes

This is the English presentation of ARCTISER’s statutes (vedtekter). The legally filed version for registration purposes may exist in Norwegian; this page reflects the same meaning in clear English for transparency.

§1 Name

The association’s name is Arctic Institute for Independent Social Research. The abbreviated name is ARCTISER.

§2 Organizational form

ARCTISER is a non-profit, non-commercial association with no profit-making purpose.

§3 Purpose

The association’s purpose is to conduct and communicate independent, desk-based social research and analysis. The work shall be based on open and existing data sources, and focus on societal, demographic, and institutional developments—particularly in northern and peripheral regions.

The association shall act professionally independent, non-political, and non-religious.

§4 Activities

The association may, among other things:

  • produce analyses, reports, and research notes
  • publish research-based material on its own platforms
  • participate in collaborations with other institutions and organizations
  • apply for public and private support in line with the purpose

§5 Membership

The board may define membership rules if the association has members. Membership does not grant any right to financial profit or distributions.

§6 Annual meeting

The annual meeting is the association’s highest authority and shall be held once per year.

The annual meeting addresses:

  • annual report
  • accounts/financial statement
  • election of the board
  • any amendments to the statutes

§7 Board

The association is governed by a board consisting of a minimum of two members. The board is responsible for the association’s operations in accordance with these statutes and applicable law.

§8 Finance

The association’s funds shall only be used to advance the association’s purpose. No members or board members have any claim to financial profit or distributions.

§9 Signature authority and representation

The board represents the association externally. The board may grant signature authority as needed.

§10 Amendments

Amendments to the statutes require a resolution at the annual meeting by a simple majority.

§11 Dissolution

Upon dissolution, the association’s remaining funds shall be transferred to another non-profit organization with a similar purpose, as decided by the annual meeting.
